Dati finanziari per: S&P 500 (GSPC)

The S&P 500 (GSPC) is one of the most widely followed stock market indices in the world. Maintained by S&P Dow Jones Indices, it tracks the performance of 500 of the largest publicly traded companies in the United States, representing approximately 80% of the total U.S. equity market capitalization. The index is market-capitalization weighted and includes companies from all major sectors, including technology, healthcare, financials, consumer discretionary, industrials, and more.

Notable companies within the S&P 500 include Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, NVIDIA, Alphabet (Google), Berkshire Hathaway, Meta Platforms, and JPMorgan Chase. Due to its broad sector coverage, the S&P 500 is considered a key benchmark for U.S. equity performance and a barometer of the overall health of the American economy. The index is rebalanced quarterly and reviewed regularly to ensure it reflects the most relevant large-cap U.S. equities.

The S&P 500 is widely used as a basis for index funds and ETFs, such as the SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY), Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO), and iShares Core S&P 500 ETF (IVV). These funds allow investors to gain diversified exposure to the U.S. market at low cost. The index is also used as a performance benchmark for active fund managers and institutional investors.

Given its diversity and size, the S&P 500 typically provides a balanced view of the U.S. stock market's growth and stability. It is also often used in the construction of portfolios for asset allocation strategies, retirement accounts, and other long-term investment vehicles. The S&P 500 was launched in 1957 and is one of the most important financial indices globally.
