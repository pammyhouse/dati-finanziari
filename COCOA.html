Dati finanziari per: Cocoa to US Dollar (COCOA)

Cocoa is a soft commodity traded on global exchanges, primarily the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) and the London International Financial Futures and Options Exchange (LIFFE). It is derived from cocoa beans, which are the primary ingredient in chocolate and other confectionery products. Cocoa futures contracts are commonly used by producers, traders, and investors to hedge against price fluctuations and speculate on market trends. The price of cocoa is influenced by various factors, including weather conditions in major producing countries (such as Ivory Coast, Ghana, and Indonesia), political stability, global demand for chocolate products, and supply chain disruptions. Additionally, currency exchange rates and speculative trading can impact price movements. As a financial asset, cocoa offers opportunities for portfolio diversification and risk management, particularly for investors looking to gain exposure to the agricultural commodities market.
