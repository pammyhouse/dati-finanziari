Dati finanziari per: Silver Futures (SI=F)

SI=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on silver traded on the COMEX exchange, part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 5,000 troy ounces of silver and is quoted in U.S. dollars per ounce. Silver is a precious metal that serves both as an investment asset and as a critical industrial input in sectors such as electronics, solar energy, medical technologies, and jewelry manufacturing. Silver futures are widely used by producers, manufacturers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price fluctuations, managing supply chain risks, and speculating on market trends. Unlike the spot price of silver, commonly represented by the XAG/USD quote, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot market due to factors such as interest rates, storage costs, and expectations about future supply and demand, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker SI=F generally reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for the global silver market. The price of silver is influenced by a wide range of factors, including global economic conditions, inflation expectations, U.S. dollar strength, industrial demand, mining supply, recycling activity, and investor sentiment, making it an important component of diversified investment portfolios and a potential hedge during periods of economic uncertainty.
