Dati finanziari per: Wheat Futures (ZW=F)

ZW=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on Chicago Soft Red Winter (SRW) wheat traded on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT), part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 5,000 bushels of wheat and is quoted in U.S. cents per bushel. Wheat is one of the world’s most important staple crops, serving as a primary ingredient in bread, pasta, and numerous food products, making it essential to global food security. Wheat futures are widely used by farmers, grain elevators, food producers, traders, and institutional investors to hedge against price volatility, manage supply risks, and speculate on agricultural market trends. Unlike spot transactions, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to storage costs, interest rates, and expectations about future harvests, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker ZW=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for global wheat prices. Price movements are influenced by weather conditions, crop yields, geopolitical events, trade policies, currency fluctuations, and global demand for food products.
