Dati finanziari per: WTI Crude Oil Futures (CL=F)

CL=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil traded on the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX), part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 1,000 barrels of light sweet crude oil and is quoted in U.S. dollars per barrel. Crude oil is a cornerstone of the global energy system and serves as the primary raw material for producing gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and a wide range of petrochemical products, making it essential to economic activity worldwide. WTI crude oil futures are widely used by energy companies, airlines, refiners, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price fluctuations, managing operational risks, and speculating on market trends. Unlike spot or over-the-counter instruments, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to factors such as storage costs, transportation constraints, interest rates, and market expectations, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker CL=F generally reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for the North American oil market. The price of crude oil is influenced by numerous factors, including geopolitical tensions, decisions by the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC and its allies), U.S. shale production, global supply and demand dynamics, refinery capacity, inventory levels reported by agencies such as the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), currency fluctuations, and overall macroeconomic conditions, making it one of the most closely watched and liquid commodities in global financial markets.
