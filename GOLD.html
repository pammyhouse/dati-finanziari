Dati finanziari per: Gold Futures (GC=F)

GC=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on gold traded on the COMEX exchange, part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 100 troy ounces of gold and is quoted in U.S. dollars per ounce. Gold futures are widely used by investors, traders, and institutions for hedging, speculation, and price discovery.

Unlike the spot price of gold (commonly represented by XAU/USD), futures contracts have expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to factors such as interest rates, storage costs, and market expectations (known as contango or backwardation). The continuous ticker GC=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract.

Gold is traditionally considered a safe-haven asset and is often sought during periods of economic uncertainty, inflation, and geopolitical tension. It is also held by central banks as a store of value and plays a key role in diversified investment portfolios.
