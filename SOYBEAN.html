Dati finanziari per: Soybean Futures (ZS=F)

ZS=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on soybeans traded on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT), part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 5,000 bushels of soybeans and is quoted in U.S. cents per bushel. Soybeans are a crucial agricultural commodity used in food products, animal feed, and the production of soybean oil and biodiesel, making them an essential component of the global food and energy supply chains. Soybean futures are widely used by farmers, food processors, biofuel producers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price volatility, managing supply risks, and speculating on agricultural market trends. Unlike spot transactions, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to storage costs, interest rates, and expectations about future harvests, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker ZS=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for global soybean prices. Market prices are influenced by weather conditions, crop yields in major producing countries such as the United States and Brazil, global demand for animal feed and vegetable oils, trade policies, currency movements, and biofuel regulations.
