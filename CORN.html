Dati finanziari per: Corn Futures (ZC=F)

ZC=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on corn traded on the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT), part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 5,000 bushels of corn and is quoted in U.S. cents per bushel. Corn is a versatile agricultural commodity used for food production, animal feed, ethanol manufacturing, and various industrial applications, making it a vital component of the global agricultural economy. Corn futures are widely utilized by farmers, agribusiness companies, ethanol producers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price fluctuations, managing supply risks, and speculating on market movements. Unlike spot market transactions, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to storage costs, financing rates, and expectations about future harvests, known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker ZC=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for global corn prices. Price dynamics are influenced by weather conditions, planting and harvest reports, biofuel policies, global demand for animal feed, export trends, and currency fluctuations.
