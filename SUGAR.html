Dati finanziari per: Sugar #11 Futures (SB=F)

SB=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on Sugar No. 11 traded on the Intercontinental Exchange (ICE). Each standard contract corresponds to 112,000 pounds of raw cane sugar and is quoted in U.S. cents per pound. Sugar is one of the most widely consumed agricultural commodities in the world and is used extensively in food and beverage production as well as in the manufacturing of ethanol, particularly in countries such as Brazil. Sugar futures are utilized by producers, refiners, food manufacturers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price fluctuations, managing supply chain risks, and speculating on market trends. Unlike spot transactions, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to storage costs, financing rates, and expectations regarding future supply and demand, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker SB=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for the global sugar market. Prices are influenced by weather conditions in major producing countries such as Brazil, India, and Thailand, government policies, biofuel demand, currency fluctuations, and global consumption patterns.
