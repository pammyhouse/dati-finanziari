Dati finanziari per: Copper Futures (HG=F)

HG=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on copper traded on the COMEX exchange, part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 25,000 pounds of high-grade copper and is quoted in U.S. dollars per pound. Copper is a key industrial metal widely used in construction, electrical wiring, renewable energy systems, transportation, and electronics, making it a critical indicator of global economic activity and often referred to as “Dr. Copper” for its ability to signal economic trends. Copper futures are extensively utilized by mining companies, manufacturers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price fluctuations, managing supply risks, and speculating on market movements. Unlike spot market transactions, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot price due to factors such as storage costs, financing rates, and expectations about future supply and demand, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker HG=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for the global copper market. Prices are influenced by global economic growth, infrastructure spending, technological innovation, mining output, inventory levels, currency movements, and geopolitical developments.
