Dati finanziari per: Natural Gas Futures (NG=F)

NG=F represents the continuous front-month futures contract on Henry Hub natural gas traded on the New York Mercantile Exchange (NYMEX), part of the CME Group. Each standard contract corresponds to 10,000 million British thermal units (MMBtu) and is quoted in U.S. dollars per MMBtu. Natural gas is a key energy commodity and one of the primary fuels used for residential and commercial heating, electricity generation, and a wide range of industrial applications, making it essential to the global energy system. Natural gas futures are widely utilized by energy producers, utilities, industrial consumers, traders, and institutional investors for hedging against price volatility, managing supply risks, and speculating on market trends. Unlike spot or over-the-counter instruments, futures contracts have specific expiration dates and may trade at a premium or discount to the spot market due to factors such as storage costs, transportation constraints, interest rates, and market expectations, a condition known as contango or backwardation. The continuous ticker NG=F typically reflects the most actively traded front-month contract, providing a reliable benchmark for the North American natural gas market. The price of natural gas is influenced by numerous factors, including seasonal weather patterns that affect heating and cooling demand, storage levels reported by the U.S. Energy Information Administration, production rates from shale gas fields, liquefied natural gas (LNG) export dynamics, geopolitical developments, regulatory and energy policy changes, and broader macroeconomic conditions, making it one of the most closely monitored and liquid commodities in global financial markets.
